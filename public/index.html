How to Play Simulated Football

START GAME:

Click the "Receive" button to receive a kick from the computer. You then can call an offensive play by clicking on one of the highlighted buttons.

If you score a TD or kick a field goal, you then click on the "Kick" button to kick the ball to the computer, which will then have possession of the ball.

When the computer has the ball, you choose either the run defense or pass defense play to simulate the computer's offensive play call.

PLAYBOOK:

Goaline Run - higher chance for gaining short yardage, but also higher chance of fumbling.

Off-tackle - run play offering lower chance of gaining postive yards, but longer positive yardage potential.

Toss/Sweep - run play with equal chances of big yardage and negative yardage potential.

Screen Pass - high chance of low yardage gains

Pass - normal pass play with lower completion percentage, but higher yardage gains

Pass Long - even lower completion percentage and higher interception risk, but potential for big yardage gains.

Run Def - When the computer is on offense, this defense protects against the run

Pass Def - This defense protects against the pass

*** When it is 4th down and you anticipate the computer punting or attempting a field goal, your defensive options remain Run Def and Pass Def to protect against a 4th down run or pass.
